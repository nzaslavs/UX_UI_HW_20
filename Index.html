Boostrap Case Study - the NODE

The NODE - a platform where users can meet others who share their interests.

Welcome to NODE, a cutting-edge UX/UI project that pushes the boundaries of design and functionality.

In the fast-paced world of digital experiences, the need for rapid prototyping and efficient design iterations is crucial.

This is where Bootstrap, the open-source front-end framework, emerges as the perfect ally for bringing the NODE project to life.

Defining the PROBLEM.

Why we call it the NODE?

Because it helps connect to a large network of people just like a computer node on the network.

We were a team of 4, and we worked as UX designers.

How we Crafted it?

We went through the 5 stages of the design process. We did user interviews to understand the user, got insights and created a user persona.

We defined the problem, came up with ideas to solve it, prototyped and tested the prototype.

Research & Development

From 5 user interviews and an online survey, we found that people prefer meeting new acquaintances through friends and family, and they are not keen on connecting with complete strangers.

However, they are open to meeting others through mutual acquaintances they trust, like a friend's friend.

Finding the SOLUTION.

Kelly Speaks

Please meet Kelly - our user persona developed with the help of our interview insights.

Kelly has a demanding job and a busy schedule, is married, she has friends, but wants to be able to meet others without going to various social gatherings. She wishes to meet people with similar interests and have meaningful interactions with them right away.

She also does not have time to waste on bots or people who just catfish online for whatever reason.

Kelly's goals are to expand her social circle with legitimate people with similar interests, and maybe learn a new skill. Her pains are a busy work schedule, difficulty trusting strangers, and finding others who would share her interests easily. Her gains would be building a strong support system and emotional well being.

Problem

With all the information we got from our research and our user persona it was easy to formulate a problem statement: “People wish to find others with similar interests but are concerned about the legitimacy of other users.

How might we build a platform where users can meet others with similar interests through their existing social circles?”

Solution

Here is our Value Proposition: Say goodbye to the uncertainty of encountering strangers. With NODE, you can confidently discover and connect with people who share your interests, values, and passions.

Unlock the potential of your friends' networks to build genuine connections and create lasting friendships.

Make friends through your friends to make a process more trustworthy and safe.
